# Pause Criteria

Pause when the author agent cannot responsibly revise with the available information. The pause should be specific and answerable.

## Pause Categories

### Additional Evidence Needed

Use when a reviewer concern requires:

- new experiments or simulations
- more replicates or uncertainty analysis
- missing raw data, images, spectra, logs, code, or notebooks
- access to a supplementary file or prior manuscript
- a model parameter, geometry, material property, or calibration detail not available

Ask for the smallest useful item, not a broad request.

### Author Judgment Needed

Use when multiple defensible paths exist:

- change target journal or scope
- remove a section vs collect new evidence
- frame a result as mechanistic, semi-empirical, or empirical
- choose whether to disclose a limitation prominently
- decide how much novelty to claim
- decide whether to include confidential or unpublished data

Give 2-3 clear options and the tradeoff for each.

### Theory Or Model Needed

Use when the revision would require:

- deriving a new theoretical model
- selecting among competing physical mechanisms without evidence
- introducing a correlation beyond its validity range
- claiming causality from correlation

Offer a safe fallback: reframe as empirical, add a limitation, or propose future work.

### Ethical, Confidential, Or Authorship Constraints

Use when revision requires:

- private reviewer text not provided by the user
- coauthor approval
- confidential data release
- human-subject, animal, clinical, or safety-sensitive decisions
- legal or patent strategy

Do not proceed by guessing.

## How To Pause

Use this format:

**Pause Needed**

I can continue after you provide or decide:

1. Specific missing item or decision.
2. Why it matters for the manuscript.
3. What I will do once it is available.

Keep the pause narrow. Do not ask for information that can be inferred, computed, or verified from available files.
