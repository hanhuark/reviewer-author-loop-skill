# Reviewer Rubric

Use this rubric to produce constructive, decision-oriented review comments. Adapt the details to the target journal or discipline.

## Decision Levels

- **Accept**: contribution is clear, evidence is sufficient, and only copyediting remains.
- **Accept after minor editorial revision**: no new evidence is required; remaining work is clarity, formatting, citations, or small caveats.
- **Minor revision**: core result is credible, but specific clarifications, checks, or presentation changes are needed.
- **Major revision**: promising contribution, but claims, methods, evidence, analysis, or structure need substantial work.
- **Reject or out of scope**: core contribution is unsupported, not novel enough, outside scope, or requires a fundamentally different study.
- **Human input required**: the manuscript may be salvageable, but the agent cannot responsibly revise without additional data, experiments, modeling, permissions, or author decisions.

## Review Dimensions

### Contribution And Fit

- Is the research question important for the target venue?
- Is the novelty explicit and credible?
- Does the manuscript explain why the result matters beyond the specific case?
- Does the title/abstract match the actual evidence?

### Evidence And Methods

- Are experimental, computational, theoretical, or analytical methods sufficiently described?
- Are assumptions, boundary conditions, materials, data sources, and validity ranges clear?
- Are uncertainty, repeatability, sensitivity, or robustness treated at a level appropriate to the claims?
- Are data reduction and model-fitting choices reproducible?

### Logic And Interpretation

- Does each major conclusion follow from the presented evidence?
- Are alternative explanations considered?
- Are mechanistic claims separated from empirical correlations?
- Are limitations stated without undermining the contribution?

### Figures, Tables, And Equations

- Can each figure stand alone with its caption?
- Are symbols, units, and subscripts consistent?
- Are plotted models fitted, predicted, or illustrative?
- Are equations defined and tied to physical meaning?

### Literature And Positioning

- Are seminal and recent sources represented?
- Are citations used to support specific claims rather than as decoration?
- Are similar studies compared fairly?
- Are borrowed models or correlations used within their validity range?

### Writing And Structure

- Does the introduction reach the gap early enough?
- Does the methods section enable reproduction?
- Are results organized by argument rather than data dump?
- Does the conclusion match the evidence and avoid overclaiming?

## Review Output Pattern

Use this order:

1. **Recommendation**
2. **Summary Of Contribution**
3. **Major Comments**
4. **Minor Comments**
5. **Required Changes Before Acceptance**
6. **Optional Suggestions**

Make comments actionable. A good comment tells the author what is wrong, why it matters, and what evidence or revision would resolve it.
